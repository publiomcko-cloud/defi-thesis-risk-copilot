# Case Study — DeFi Thesis & Risk Copilot

## 1. Problem

DeFi users often evaluate strategies using surface-level metrics such as APY or TVL. This can hide major risks.

A strategy may look attractive because the displayed yield is high, but the real risk may come from:

- variable borrow rates
- low secondary market liquidity
- volatile collateral
- liquidation thresholds
- oracle dependencies
- maturity constraints
- incentive emissions
- composability across multiple protocols

The problem is not only lack of data. The problem is fragmented interpretation.

## 2. Product Idea

DeFi Thesis & Risk Copilot is an AI research assistant that transforms fragmented protocol documentation, public market data, strategy parameters, and risk rules into structured reports.

The system does not execute trades. It helps users reason about strategy quality, risk, uncertainty, and monitoring requirements.

## 3. Initial Domain

The initial domain focuses on:

- Pendle fixed-yield and PT strategies
- Morpho lending markets
- Aave lending and borrowing risk
- cross-protocol loop strategies
- liquidity, oracle, borrow rate, and liquidation risk

## 4. Why This Project Matters

This project demonstrates practical AI engineering in a domain where simple chatbots are not enough.

The application must combine:

- document retrieval
- structured data
- calculations
- risk frameworks
- LLM reasoning
- careful disclaimers
- uncertainty handling
- clear UI presentation

## 5. Technical Challenge

The main technical challenge is designing a system that does not simply hallucinate a DeFi opinion.

The system must:

1. retrieve relevant sources
2. normalize available data
3. identify missing data
4. apply deterministic risk rules
5. generate a report with citations and assumptions
