# Pendle Notes

## Principal Tokens

Pendle separates yield-bearing assets into Principal Tokens, often called PTs, and Yield Tokens. A PT represents the right to redeem the underlying asset at maturity. PT strategies are often evaluated through fixed-yield concepts because the market price of the PT can imply a return if held until maturity.

## Maturity Risk

Maturity risk appears when a strategy depends on holding a PT until a specific date or exiting through a secondary market before maturity. Exit timing, secondary market liquidity, slippage, and changing implied yields can materially affect the realized outcome.

## Liquidity Considerations

Pendle positions can depend on pool liquidity and market depth. Low liquidity can increase slippage, make early exits harder, and increase uncertainty around displayed implied APY.
